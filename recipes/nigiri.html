Nigiri

Description

Do you know what the difference is between maki and nigiri? Well, nigiri isn't rolled. Instead, it is comprised of a thin slice of raw or cooked seafood atop a mound of vinegary rice. Each piece of nigiri is eaten whole in one bite. The difference between sashimi and nigiri is that sashimi has no rice, but only the thin slice of raw or cooked fish.

Many people believe that the fish is the most important part of the dish. However, many famous Japanese sushi chefs say that the most important is the rice and not the fish. In Japan, many trainees who aspire to become sushi chef often only learn how to prepare the rice first for up to 10 years! Whenever you eat sushi, it is imperative to use fresh fish unless you like having an upset stomach! With greater risk, there is a bigger payout!

Ingredients

Homemade Nigiri Staples

  • Sushi Rice
  • Sushi Vinegar
  • Wasabi
  • Pickled Ginger
  • Soy Sauce
  • Eel Sauce
  • Nori (Seaweed Sheet)
  • Sharp Knife

Favorite Fish

  • Salmon
  • Tuna
  • Ebi
  • Hamachi
  • Eel
  • Tako
  • Uni

Steps

  1. Cook the rice
  2. Season the rice
  3. Slice the fish
  4. Shape the rice
  5. Assemble the fish and the rice
  6. You may add a thin strip of nori around each piece of nigiri.
